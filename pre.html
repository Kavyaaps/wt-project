How to Handle Stamps to Best Preserve Them

Did you know that touching your stamp bare-handed can damage them? That’s because fingertips secrete oils that can affect your stamp collection. That’s why it’s important to handle your stamps correctly with the right tools. Here are some essentials:

  • Handling gloves: Lint-free cotton gloves protect stamps from oil and moisture.
  • Magnifying glass: Helps examine stamp details and check for damage.
  • Watermark detector fluid: Reveals hidden watermarks on stamps.
  • Perforation gauge: Measures the holes on the stamp edges to identify them accurately.
  • Color guide: Helps identify and catalog different stamp colors.
  • Storage cases: Albums or other storage methods keep stamps organized and safe.
  • Stamp tongs: Essential for picking up stamps without touching them directly.

Environmental factors to watch

Humidity

Keep humidity between 35–55% to prevent warping and mold. A dehumidifier can help maintain this level.

Temperature

Maintain temperatures between 65–72°F. Extreme heat, cold, or fluctuations can damage stamps.

Sunlight

Avoid direct sunlight to prevent fading, cracking, and discoloration. Store stamps in dark places.

How to Store Stamps

Choosing the right storage method is crucial to protect your collection.

Stamp albums

Designed for stamps from a specific country or theme, with dedicated slots and descriptions to keep things organized.

Stockbooks

Offer flexibility to arrange stamps however you want. Great for custom collections and adding notes.

How to mount stamps

Methods to attach stamps to albums or stockbooks:

  • Stamp hinges: Folded paper or plastic. Easy to use but may leave residue; best for experienced collectors.
  • Stamp mounts: Clear plastic sleeves. Protect mint stamps better but cost more.

Using the right supplies and storage methods ensures your stamp collection stays beautiful and valuable. Happy stamp collecting!
